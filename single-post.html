Proposes stricter rules for foreign dental treatment

The incidence of antibiotic-resistant bacteria is increasing sharply. Stricter rules are now being proposed, including for dental treatment abroad to prevent Norwegians from taking resistant bacteria home.

We know that a lot of people travel and get dental treatments abroad to save money, and come back with multi-resistant bacteria, says Kari Lise Eidjar. The doctor at Emergency Room West tells of three patients in recent months who became infected after treatment in Thailand, the Philippines and Bulgaria. Norwegians today use twice as much antibiotics as in the 1970s. Although use has declined somewhat in recent years, overconsumption is a major health threat because it leads to resistant bacteria. In addition, we travel much more.

- I think that perhaps one should have a political discussion related to whether it is okay for Norwegians to travel and receive treatments abroad, asks Eidjar, who also wonders whether lowering the prices of Norwegian dental services can help prevent more patients from traveling abroad.

Large treatments give great risk

Camilla Hansen Steinum, president of the Danish Dental Association, says it is a well-known problem that Norwegian patients are infected with resistant bacteria abroad.

We're still hearing about this. Especially patients who receive massive, surgical treatment abroad as implant treatment, run a high risk of becoming infected.

She says they are difficult to warn patients in general against treatments abroad because there are big differences between countries and treatment. - But the further south in Europe you go, the greater the risk of contracting the infection. According to Steinum, it is difficult to regulate those who travel of their own free will and pay for themselves. - But there are some who receive social security support to perform the dental treatment abroad, and then we believe that it should be discussed whether this is something the Norwegian state should support, Steinum says. Eurodent Dental Clinic is one of the major players in treating patients in Hungary, among other things.
